A study into the nature of Christianity (and religion in general) by the German philosopher Ludwig Feuerbach, a student of Hegel. This book provides a critique of Christianity which strongly influenced generations of later thinkers, including Karl Marx and Frederich Engels.

The German original dates from 1841; this is the English translation of the second edition, 1855, reprinted in 1890.

The first pages with advertisements are somewhat hard to read, due to the small print, and have OCR-ed badly; the remainder should be fairly straightforward. Some Latin required, but not enough to mark it as "with Latin".
